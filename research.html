My research focuses on the development and validation of novel numerical models in wind energy applications. Details of certain application-related research streams can be expanded below, including selected publications.


Wind-wave interaction

Currently, my research focuses on developing a wavying boundary capability within nalu-wind for DNS/LES of wind wave-interaction

This research is funded through HFM-Exawind Projects

High-fidelity, physics-based modelling of offshore wind farms

I am interested in wind farm wake modelling. During my PhD, I developed a wind farm simulator WInc3D.

Atmospheric turbulence and very flexible aircraft (VFA)

During my postdoc at Imperial College London, I collaborated with Professor Rafael Palacios and Dr Alfonso Carre to investigate the effect of low-altitude atmospheric turbulence effects on very flexible aircraft. This research stream led to two high-impact pubications

Turbulence modelling

I am interested in turbulence modelling including closure models for the Reynolds-Averaged Navier-Stokes equations (RANS) and subgrid-scale (SGS) modelling within large-eddy simulation. Here is some work done on SGS modelling and its application to different fluid problems
